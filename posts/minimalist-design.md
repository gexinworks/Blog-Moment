Minimalism isn't about removing things. It's about making room for what matters.

## Less, but Better

Dieter Rams' principle of **"Weniger, aber besser"** remains the north star of thoughtful design. In a world drowning in visual noise, restraint is the ultimate luxury.

### The Three Pillars

1. **Space** — Let elements breathe. White space isn't empty; it's *active*.
2. **Typography** — When visuals are stripped away, type must carry the weight.
3. **Hierarchy** — Every pixel must earn its place.

> "Perfection is achieved, not when there is nothing more to add, but when there is nothing left to take away." — Antoine de Saint-Exupéry

## Typography as Architecture

Consider how a simple change in typeface transforms meaning:

- **Serif fonts** evoke tradition, authority, and timelessness
- **Sans-serif fonts** suggest modernity, clarity, and efficiency
- **Monospace fonts** imply precision, code, and raw data

The interplay between these creates rhythm — a visual heartbeat that guides the reader's eye.

## Color with Purpose

A minimalist palette doesn't mean monochrome. It means *intentional*:

```css
:root {
  --ink: #1A1A1A;       /* Primary text */
  --accent: #C4553A;    /* Single accent */
  --whisper: #FAF6F0;   /* Breathing room */
}
```

Three colors. Infinite expression.
